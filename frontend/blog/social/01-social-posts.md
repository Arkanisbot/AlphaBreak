## Reddit (r/investing, r/StockMarket, r/options)

**Title**: Why most new traders lose money — the structural reasons no one talks about

**Post**:

Throwaway-ish post but I've been working on this for a while and wanted to share.

Everyone knows the stat: 70-90% of retail traders lose money. The standard explanations are "they're undisciplined" or "they don't have a strategy."

I think those explanations are incomplete. The real reasons are structural, and understanding them is the first step toward not being in that 70-90%:

**1. You're starting with a -1% to -4% annual deficit before any trades**

Bid-ask spreads. Slippage. Payment for order flow. Even with "zero commission" brokers, these costs compound. For active traders, transaction costs eat 1-4% per year. Your strategy must beat the market plus friction.

**2. Institutional information asymmetry is more subtle than people think**

It's not about insider trading. It's that institutions have statistical models on decades of data, teams of researchers, and execution systems that operate in milliseconds. The data is mostly public — the *processing* is what's different. You can't beat them by being smarter at chart reading. You can only beat them by exploiting the constraints they face (capacity limits, mandate restrictions, time horizon mismatch).

**3. Loss aversion is real and it's destroying your account**

This is empirically established (Kahneman/Tversky). Humans feel losses about 2x more intensely than equivalent gains. The behavioral consequence: cutting winners early ("don't lose the gains") and holding losers too long ("wait for the bounce"). Result: small wins, huge losses, negative expectancy even if you're right more than 50% of the time.

The traders who survive aren't smarter. They've built systems that account for these forces:
- Pre-defined risk per trade (1-2% rule)
- Written theses that make invalidation explicit
- Journaling to separate skill from luck across hundreds of trades
- Acceptance that losses are normal expenses, not personal failures
